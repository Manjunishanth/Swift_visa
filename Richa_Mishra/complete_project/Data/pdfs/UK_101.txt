# 1. Skilled Worker Visa Eligibility

The UK Skilled Worker visa allows individuals to work for a licensed UK employer in an eligible skilled occupation.

## 1.1 Job Eligibility (Skill Requirements)

A job qualifies as a ‚ÄúSkilled Worker‚Äù role if all of the following are true:

* The job must appear in the **Home Office ‚ÄúAppendix Skilled Occupations‚Äù** list.
* It must be at **RQF Level 3** or above (equivalent to A-level or higher).
* The employer must be a **Home Office‚Äìapproved sponsor**.
* The applicant must have a valid **Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS)** specifying:

  * job title
  * occupation code
  * salary
  * work address
  * start and end dates

### 1.1.1 Examples of Skilled Occupations (non-exhaustive)

* Software Developer
* Data Analyst
* Civil Engineer
* Mechanical Engineer
* Accountant
* Nurse
* Healthcare worker roles (various)
* Secondary school teacher
* Electrician
* Marketing Associate (if SOC code is approved)

Roles like **driver**, **cashier**, **general laborer**, **waiter**, **receptionist**, or **delivery rider** are *not* eligible unless tied to a specific approved SOC code.

# 1.2 Salary Eligibility

To qualify, the salary must meet **whichever is highest** of:

1. **General salary threshold:**

* ¬£38,700 per year (current standard threshold)

2. **The ‚Äúgoing rate‚Äù** for the job‚Äôs SOC code

* Each occupation code has its own minimum salary (hourly or annual).
* Some technical and STEM jobs have lower going rates.

3. **Hourly minimum**

* Must be at least ¬£11.90/hour unless exempt.

### 1.2.1 Cases where lower salaries are allowed

Lower salary thresholds apply only if the applicant meets specific exceptions:

* **New entrant to the labor market**

  * under age 26
  * switching from student visa
  * within 2 years of graduation
    ‚Üí threshold may reduce to **70% of the going rate**.

* **STEM PhD relevant to the role**
  ‚Üí may use 80‚Äì90% of the going rate.

* **Health & Care visa route**
  ‚Üí Reduced salary thresholds apply for eligible NHS roles.

If none of the above apply, the **full salary** rules must be met.

# 1.3 Education Eligibility

* Minimum requirement: **At least RQF Level 3 skills**, not necessarily a degree.
* For many roles (IT, engineering, science, finance, construction), a **bachelor‚Äôs degree or equivalent experience** is standard.
* Some roles require **regulated qualifications**, e.g.:

  * nurses ‚Üí NMC registration
  * teachers ‚Üí QTS in some cases
  * doctors ‚Üí GMC registration

---

# 1.4 English Language Requirement

Must meet one of:

* IELTS UKVI (minimum CEFR level B1 or B2 depending on route)
* Degree taught in English (verified via ECCTIS)
* Nationality from an English-speaking country (not applicable to India)

---

# 1.5 Financial/Personal Funds

Applicant must show they can support themselves **unless** their sponsor certifies maintenance.

Standard requirement:

* **¬£1,270** in bank account for 28 days (if not sponsored for maintenance).
* Additional funds apply for dependents.

---

# 1.6 Job Offer Requirements

The job offer must:

* Come from a **licensed sponsor**
* Match an **eligible SOC code**
* Meet **salary threshold**
* Include a **Certificate of Sponsorship**

---

# 1.7 Health Requirements

* Mandatory **TB test certificate** for applicants from India.
* Must pay **Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)** (except Health & Care Visa).
* Some roles require medical clearance (e.g., healthcare workers).

---

# 1.8 Age Requirements

No strict minimum/maximum age, but:

* ‚ÄúNew entrant‚Äù salary benefits apply only if **under 26** OR a recent graduate.

---

# 1.9 Factors That Affect Eligibility

* Criminal record (serious offenses may cause refusal)
* Prior visa compliance history
* Gaps in employment documentation
* Mismatched job titles and SOC codes
* Insufficient salary or incorrect job code
* Failure to prove English language proficiency

---

# 1.10 When Skilled Worker Visa Is Not Possible

Not eligible if:

* Job is not in an eligible SOC code
* Salary is below the required threshold
* Employer is not licensed
* Incorrect or missing CoS
* Wrong or invalid TB test
* Cannot meet English language requirement

# 2. Health and Care Worker Visa (Special Route)

A simplified version of Skilled Worker for health professionals.

## 2.1 Occupations Eligible

Examples:

* Nurses
* Doctors
* Healthcare assistants (if on eligible SOC code)
* Physiotherapists
* Radiographers
* Paramedics
* Lab technicians (health-related)

## 2.2 Benefits

* Reduced salary threshold
* Reduced visa fees
* **No Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)**
* Fast-track decision processing

# 3. Student Visa Eligibility (UK)

For full-time study at an approved institution.

## 3.1 Education Requirements

* CAS (Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies) from licensed institution
* Valid academic offer
* Course must meet minimum study level requirements (RQF Level 3+)

## 3.2 Financial Requirements

Required funds:

* Tuition fees for year 1
* Living costs:

  * **¬£1,334/month** in London
  * **¬£1,023/month** outside London
* Must show financial proof **for 28 days**
* Funds must be in an acceptable financial institution

## 3.3 English Requirement

* IELTS UKVI (normally CEFR B2 for degree courses)
* Equivalent approved tests accepted

## 3.4 Other Conditions

* TB test required
* Parental consent required if under 18
* No full-time work allowed (only limited hours)

# 5. Visitor Visa (Tourist/Short Stay)

## 5.1 General Eligibility

* Purpose must be tourism, business meetings, medical treatment, or short study
* Must show intent to return
* Must show sufficient funds for stay
* Invitation letter (optional but helpful)

## 5.2 Financial Requirements

No fixed amount, but must show:

* Bank balance supporting the trip
* Accommodation plan
* Travel funds

## 5.3 Restrictions

Cannot:

* Work
* Switch to another visa
* Marry (unless using Marriage Visitor Visa)

## 6.2 Documents Needed
