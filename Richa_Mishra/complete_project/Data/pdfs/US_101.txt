## 1. Overview

The H-1B visa requires the employer to:

* Offer a **specialty occupation** job.
* Pay at least the **prevailing wage**.
* Ensure the beneficiary meets all **qualification criteria**.

This document provides general rules used by USCIS and the U.S. Department of Labor (DOL).
It does **not** contain exact wage numbers (because they vary by location and change yearly).

## 4. Prevailing Wage Requirement (General Rules)

### The employer must pay **the higher of**:

* The **actual wage** paid to similar employees at the company, OR
* The **prevailing wage** determined by DOL for that **occupation + location + level**.

### Factors that affect prevailing wage:

1. **Job location (city/county/MSA)**

   * Large tech hubs have higher wages.
2. **Wage Level (1â€“4)** based on:

   * Required experience
   * Job complexity
   * Level of supervision
3. **SOC code**

### General Wage Level Meaning (Conceptual Only)

* **Level 1 (Entry)** â€“ Basic tasks, close supervision.
* **Level 2 (Qualified)** â€“ Moderate experience.
* **Level 3 (Experienced)** â€“ Advanced duties.

## 5. General Salary Expectations (Safe, Non-Numeric Range Explanation)

To avoid hallucinating any specific wage values, the following are **accurate generalizations**:

* In **high-cost cities** (e.g., San Francisco, NYC, Seattle), prevailing wages for software roles are **significantly higher** than in mid-cost areas.

## 1. Why Salary Matters for H-1B

For H-1B visas, the employer must pay at least the **â€œactual wageâ€** or the **â€œprevailing wageâ€**, whichever is higher.

* Actual wage: what current employees in similar positions earn at the same employer.
* Prevailing wage: set by U.S. Department of Labor (DOL) based on job location, SOC code, experience level (Level 1â€“4).

Because prevailing wages vary significantly by geography, industry, and job level, there is **no fixed universal wage threshold**. Instead we can reason using **salary bands**.

## 4. When Wage Must Be Upper-Mid or Higher

Wage should be in **Upper-Mid / High / Top Band** if:

* Job location is a **high-cost metro** (e.g., San Francisco Bay Area, New York City, Seattle, Boston, Los Angeles).
* Role demands **specialized or senior-level skills** (senior engineer, architect, management, specialized niche).
* Experience level is high (senior, 5+ years).
