Schengen Visa Screening Requirements - Detailed
Overview
This document summarises the short-stay (Schengen 'C') visa screening and requirements across
the Schengen Area, based on the EU Visa Code and European Commission guidance.
Schengen visa processing is coordinated across member states; biometric data is shared through
the Visa Information System (VIS).
Primary Identity & Application Forms
Valid passport with sufficient validity and blank pages, completed short-stay visa application form
for the relevant member state.
Recent passport photographs complying with Schengen photo rules.
VIS & Biometrics
Schengen states collect biometric identifiers — a photograph and 10 fingerprints — stored in the
Visa Information System (VIS) for up to 5 years to support identity verification and to detect fraud.
Biometrics reduce identity fraud and speed up border checks across member states.
